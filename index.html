An increase in type-2 diabetes and sugar consumption

More people than ever before are being diagnosed with type-2 diabetes. There is also a clear increase over time in our consumption of sugars.

Sweets on the rise

While we historically did purchase more raw sugar, this also implies that people had to cook their own sweets. Today no one has to go through this hassle since soft drinks and sweets are available everywhere. This shift in consumption is clearly seen in the visualization on the left.

The data also shows a clear trend shift, where the consumption of added sugars now mainly comes from soft drinks. Public health data also shows that 80% of our sugar consumption comes from the sugar added in ready-made foods and drinks. Looking at the data, it becomes clear that soft drinks are a dominant source of added sugar.

Diabetes on the rise

Diabetes is still rising quickly in Sweden, even though the pace has slowed down a bit. Among those diagnosed, 90% of these are diagnosed with an unhealthy lifestyle as the main cause.

Furthermore, the Swedish government suspects that a third of today's cases are still undiagnosed. Currently, about 4% of the Swedish population already have diabetes. At the current pace, the number of affected Swedes could rise to a million by 2050.

How does age relate to diabetes diagnoses?

The amount of diabetes patients is most prevalent among the elderly, tough there is also an increase in reported cases among younger people. People under 20 have over four times more cases of diabetes than those aged 20-44. Meanwhile, there is also an increase across all age groups, though only a marginal one for the age-group 20-44.

Diabetes effects our bodies

The excess blood sugar in diabetes can wreak havoc on blood vessels all over the body and cause complications. It can severely damage the eyes, kidneys, nerves, and other body parts; cause sexual problems; and double the risk of heart attack and stroke.

Examples of effects on our bodies

Cardiovascular disease:
Diabetes dramatically increases the risk of various cardiovascular problems, including coronary artery disease with chest pain (angina), heart attack, stroke and narrowing of arteries (atherosclerosis).

Nerve damage (neuropathy):
Excess sugar can injure the walls of the tiny blood vessels (capillaries) that nourish your nerves, especially in your legs.

Depression:
Depression symptoms are common in people with type 1 and type 2

Diving into the consumption

As seen above, sugar consumption and diabetes cases are on the rise. Meanwhile, one has to be careful about assuming that there is a correlation, solely based on this fact. Furthermore, the data below shows that there is a huge difference between the highest consuming 5% and the lowest consuming 5% for both men and women. Where the highest consuming men and women both eat more than 400% above the daily recommendation.

We eat more sugar compared to 50 years ago and diabetes diagnoses are increasing. Meanwhile, it is expected that almost 1 million Swedes will have diabetes by 2050. Staying healthy, in different ways, is the key factor to reduce the risk of diabetes, and eating less sugars is one way to stay healthy.
